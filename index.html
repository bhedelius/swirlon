The physics

Each particle obeys a Langevin equation v̇ = f + (b−v²)v + √(2D)·ξ. The term (b−v²)v is self-propulsion: it pumps a particle up to speed √b in whatever direction it points. Crucially there is no alignment — unlike flocking models, neighbours don't copy each other's heading.

There's no explicit fluid — this is dry active matter. The propulsion term is itself the coupling to an implicit substrate: +b·v is a negative friction that feeds energy in (the motor), while −v²·v is ordinary friction that takes it out. Their balance fixes the free-particle speed at √b. It's not a vacuum (nothing would set a preferred speed) and not a passive solvent (that could only remove energy); momentum isn't conserved — the substrate absorbs it.

The interaction f has a hard core and a short-range attraction — this range is the whole trick. Long-range attraction lets a cluster feel a smooth pull toward its centre and simply translate together (a flock — try the Flock preset). Short, local attraction frustrates that: a bound particle still must move at √b but is hemmed in by neighbours, so the cluster resolves the conflict by circulating around its own centre of mass. That collective orbit is a swirlon; roughly half spin clockwise, half counter-clockwise.

Coherent rotation takes time to build. Fresh from disorder, small clusters churn and some briefly flock; give it ~20–30 seconds and they coarsen into larger swirlons that lock into a clean spin (about half each way). Watch the velocity arrows curl around each centre. Noise sets the trade-off: low noise (default) → clean, coherent spin but swirlons slowly coalesce; more noise (try the Churn preset) → particles boil off as fast as they join, giving a steady population that forms and breaks — at the cost of crisp rotation.

The counter-intuitive payoff: a swirlon responds to a steady force (the External nudge) by moving at constant velocity, not constant acceleration — active drag makes a cluster behave as if immersed in a viscous fluid. Weaken attraction for a gas, crowd the box for churning liquid drops, and cut propulsion for a frozen solid. The box wraps around (periodic).
